Facts about Poverty

1. Global Prevalence

As of 2021, approximately 689 million people, or 9.2% of the world's population, live on less than $1.90 a day, which is considered the international poverty line.

2. Impact on Education

Children from low-income families are less likely to attend school and complete their education. An estimated 258 million children and youth were out of school in 2018.

3. Health Disparities

Poverty is closely linked to health outcomes. People living in poverty often have limited access to healthcare services, leading to higher rates of chronic diseases and lower life expectancy.

Did You Know?

Poverty affects people in every country, and it can have a lasting impact on generations.
